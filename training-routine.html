Daily Training Routine: How to Practice Like a Pro

By Abhijith SP

Introduction

Professional Valorant players don't become champions through casual play. They follow structured training routines designed to develop specific skills and maintain peak performance. This guide outlines a daily practice schedule that mirrors pro player training regimens.

The Pro Practice Framework

Elite players dedicate 6-8 hours daily to competitive gaming, structured into focused practice sessions. Each session targets specific skill development:

  • Aim training and mechanics (1-1.5 hours)
  • Ranked matchmaking (2-3 hours)
  • Team scrimmages (2-3 hours)
  • VOD review and analysis (1 hour)

This structure maximizes learning while maintaining focus and preventing burnout.

Phase 1: Warm-Up & Aim Training (30 minutes)

What to Do: Start your session with aim trainer warmups. Use Valorant's practice range or AimLab to target specific weapon spray patterns and flick accuracy.

Exercises:

  • Free-for-all deathmatch (warm up reflexes)
  • Spray control drills (Phantom and Vandal patterns)
  • Flick shots (random targets)
  • Tracking practice (moving targets)

Spend 30 minutes here before ranked play. Your warm-up determines how sharp your aim is during competitive matches.

Phase 2: Ranked Grinding (2-3 hours)

Objective: Play with focus on game sense and decision-making, not just mechanics.

Key Areas:

  • Crosshair placement and pre-aiming
  • Map control and positioning
  • Economy management
  • Teammate communication
  • Reading enemy patterns

Play with intention. After each match, mentally note what you did well and what needs improvement. Take a 5-minute break every hour.

Phase 3: Team Scrimmages (2-3 hours)

Why This Matters: Ranked play teaches individual skill, but team scrimmages teach coordination, meta execution, and high-pressure performance.

Focus Areas:

  • Executing team strategies
  • Communication during executes
  • Adapting to enemy playstyles
  • Playing under pressure

If you're not on a team, join competitive 5-stack groups or participate in online tournaments.

Phase 4: VOD Review (30-60 minutes)

The Learning Phase: Review your matches and identify mistakes. This is where massive improvements happen.

What to Analyze:

  • Deaths — Why did you die? What angle was wrong?
  • Positioning — Could you have been in a better spot?
  • Utility Usage — Did you waste abilities?
  • Decision-Making — Should you have pushed or held?

Spend time on losses more than wins. That's where improvement lives.

Sample Daily Schedule

9:00 AM - 9:30 AM: Aim training and warmup
9:30 AM - 12:30 PM: Ranked grind (3 hours)
12:30 PM - 1:30 PM: Lunch break
1:30 PM - 4:30 PM: Team scrimmages (3 hours)
4:30 PM - 5:30 PM: VOD review and analysis
5:30 PM +: Streaming/content creation or rest

Adjust based on your schedule, but maintain consistent focus during each training phase.

Rest and Recovery

Pro players take 1-2 days off per week to recover. Mental fatigue decreases decision-making quality. If you're playing tired, you're forming bad habits. Rest improves performance.

Additionally, proper sleep (8 hours), nutrition, and exercise directly impact gaming performance. Don't neglect these fundamentals.
